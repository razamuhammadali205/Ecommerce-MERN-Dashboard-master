Consumer Health Data Notice

Notice to residents of Washington and other states with health data privacy laws.

What is Consumer Health Data?

Consumer Health Data includes personal information that relates to your past, present, or future physical or mental health. This may include information about skin conditions, allergies, or wellness preferences you share with us.

Do We Collect Consumer Health Data?

Yes, we may collect limited health-related information when you:

  • Complete a skin quiz or routine builder
  • Submit a product review mentioning skin concerns
  • Contact customer service about reactions or sensitivities
  • Book a virtual consultation with an aesthetician

Examples include: skin type, concerns (acne, rosacea, eczema), ingredient sensitivities, or pregnancy status.

How We Use Health Data

We use this data only to:

  • Recommend suitable products and routines
  • Provide personalized customer support
  • Improve our product formulations and educational content
  • Comply with safety reporting obligations

We do not use health data for advertising or profiling without your explicit consent.

Do We Share Health Data?

We do not sell your health data. We may share it only with:

  • Service providers (e.g., IT vendors, analytics) under strict confidentiality agreements
  • Healthcare professionals during consultations (with your consent)
  • Regulatory authorities if required by law

Your Health Data Rights

Under Washington’s My Health My Data Act and similar laws, you have the right to:

  • Access your health data
  • Delete your health data
  • Withdraw consent for future collection or use
  • Opt out of any sharing or processing

To exercise these rights, email healthdata@skinceuticels.com or use our Privacy Request Portal.

Data Security & Retention

We protect health data with encryption, access controls, and regular audits. We retain it only as long as needed for the purposes above or as required by law (typically up to 7 years for safety records).
